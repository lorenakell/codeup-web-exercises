FAQ about national parks:

The National Park Service protects how many acres?
A: The National Park Service protects over 84 million acres of wild landscapes and historic sites.
Which park is the oldest in the U.S.?
A: Yellowstone National Park (in Wyoming, Idaho, and Montana) is the oldest U.S. national park, founded in 1872.
Where are the 10 highest waterfalls?
A: Three of the 10 highest waterfalls in the world are located in Yosemite National Park in California.
How much taller is Ribbon Falls compared to Niagra Falls?
A: Ribbon Falls in Yosemite is nine times taller than Niagara Falls.
What is the hottest temperature recorded?
A: One of the hottest temperatures on earth was recorded in 1913 in Death Valley National Park, registering 134 degrees.
What is the Grand Canyon known as?
A: The Grand Canyon, in Grand Canyon National Park in Arizona, is known as one of the Seven Natural Wonders of the World.
What are National Parks home life to?
A: National parks are home to life on a big scale: Sequoia trees are some of the world's largest living things, and Alaskan brown bears are some of the world's largest carnivores.
Who or what is General Sherman?
A: California's Sequoia National Park is home to the largest living single-stem tree in the world, the wonderfully named General Sherman. The tree is approximately 275 feet tall and weighs approximately 1,900 metric tons.
What happened when a tree fell in 1937 in Sequoia National Park?
A: When a giant sequoia tree fell and blocked a road in Sequoia National Park in California in 1937, the National Park Service simply created a tunnel through the 275-foot by 21-foot tree.
Which National Park is the largest?
A: Wrangell-St. Elias National Park, Alaska, is the largest national park, covering 13,000 square miles or 13.2 million acres.

Big Bend

  • Big Bend National Park covers a total area of 801,163 acres.
  • It is the only state park to have an entire mountain range within its borders.
  • The park is named the “big bend” after the Rio Grande
  • The Big bend is famous for Hot Springs

Guadalupe Mountains

  • The highest peak in the Guadalupe Mountains is appropriately named Guadalupe Peak. At 8,751 feet it is the highest point in the entire state of Texas.
  • Reptiles are plentiful in the Chihuahuan Desert landscape, with snakes including bullsnakes, coachwhips, and 5 types of rattlesnakes, the largest being the western diamondback.
  • The Guadalupe Mountains were witness to the greatest mass extinction of all time.
  • There’s gold in them thar hills…or so the story goes.

Haleakalā

  • Haleakala translates to “House of the Sun”. Viewing the sunrise from the summit of Haleakala is a popular activity.
  • The only native mammals in Haleakala are bats and seals. All other land mammals were brought by man, and many have been highly destructive.
  • There are at least 1,000 native species of flowering plants in Hawaii. 90% of these are endemic, found only in Hawaii.
  • The world famous scenic Hana Highway leads to the Kipahulu coastal area of the park.
